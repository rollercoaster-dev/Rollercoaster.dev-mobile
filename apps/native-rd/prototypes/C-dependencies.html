C: Dependencies — what does a waiting step show, and where does "next" go?

Marker question: what does a step with a not-yet-satisfied dependency show — "after / depends on ⟨step⟩" (internal) and "waiting on ⟨who⟩ · expected ⟨date⟩" (external), never "blocked by"? Three treatments below: inline, chip, connector.

Task-view fork (toggle): when the next thing is waiting, does "one next step" name it & stay, or route around to the next actionable step? Both built — the register leaves this open. Nothing is ever blocked, hidden, dimmed, or disabled.
