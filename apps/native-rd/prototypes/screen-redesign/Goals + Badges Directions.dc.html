Goals tab & Badges tab · audit + directions
Two tabs, one screen — pull them apart by their jobs
In the source, GoalsScreen and BadgesScreen are the same component: purple ScreenHeader → FlatList → EmptyState. Only the row differs. But their jobs are opposite: Goals = a living worklist (few, in progress, “what do I do next”) and Badges = a trophy collection (potentially many, finished, “look what I made”). Three directions, escalating in boldness — we can always dial back.

The badge artwork — the thing we just made the hero on Detail — is a 54px thumbnail in a text row. No sense of a collection, no count, the verifiable signal is gone, and it won’t feel special at 2 badges or scale at 40.
⚠ Friction
1 · Twins. Same header, same list, same card silhouette. The two primary tabs are visually interchangeable.
2 · Opposite jobs, same shape. Goals is do; Badges is done. One should feel like work in motion, the other like a shelf of trophies.
3 · The artifact is demoted. After the celebratory Detail screen, the list shows the badge as a tiny thumbnail — the payoff deflates on the way back.
4 · No scale, no collection. Many badges are coming. A flat equal-weight list has no count, no grouping, no “wall” — and no verifiable signal.
5 · No momentum. Goals doesn’t surface the one next action, so the worklist doesn’t actually drive the next move.
6 · Generic empty states. The first-goal and first-badge moments — huge for this audience — are a shared title/body/button.
The frame
Make Goals feel like momentum — surface the next move. Make Badges feel like a growing, celebratory collection that scales and carries the verifiable signal. Keep them siblings in the Full Ride language, but never confusable.
DIRECTION A Same DNA, opposite energy — both stay lists
The lightest split, easiest to dial back: both remain vertical lists, but each card gets a clear identity. Goals leads with the next action (cool, in-motion) under a “3 in progress” count. Badges becomes a celebratory artifact card — bigger badge, warm tint, earned-date in mono, a green verifiable tick — under a “12 earned” count. They rhyme; you never confuse them.

Why this

Watch-outs

DIRECTION B Worklist vs. trophy grid — different shapes for different jobs
Commit to the difference. Goals stays a worklist but gains a “NEXT UP” hero — the single most actionable step, front and center — over compact goal rows. Badges becomes a 2-up grid trophy wall under a big count + verifiable-streak header, scaling cleanly to dozens. The tabs now look like different kinds of place.

PICK How far do you want to pull them apart?
A · Same DNA, opposite energy. Both stay lists; each card gets a clear identity (cool/action vs. warm/artifact) + a count. Safest, fastest, easiest to dial back.
B · Worklist vs. trophy grid. Goals gets a “Next up” hero; Badges becomes a celebratory grid that scales to dozens. The honest, balanced answer to “they’re the same.”
C · Cockpit vs. wall of proof. Goals = a single-focus momentum cockpit; Badges = an immersive dark gallery. Boldest; built for few goals + many badges.
My lean given “many badges, few goals”: B as the base (the grid is the right home for a growing collection), optionally borrowing C’s count header / latest-spotlight for Badges.
