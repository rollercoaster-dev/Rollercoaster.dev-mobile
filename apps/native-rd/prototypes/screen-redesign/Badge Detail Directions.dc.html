⚠ Friction · tied to the brief
1 · The hero is buried. The artifact floats, then a long utilitarian scroll follows. The payoff of a whole goal reads like a settings page.
2 · The proof is demoted. Evidence — the heart of the app — is one block under About inside a shared card, not the spine.
3 · Three exports + a caption. Near-identical actions = decision load for this audience. No single confident share.
4 · Flat hierarchy. View timeline, 3 exports, Delete are all stacked same-weight buttons. Delete shouldn’t sit beside Share.
5 · Degraded states are bare. Undesigned, no narrative, soft-deleted goal — the page just drops blocks with no celebration left.
6 · The credential is invisible. “Verifiable, portable” — the real differentiator — lives only inside a button label and a caption.
The frame
Badge = celebrated hero. Evidence = the spine. Three exports → one confident Share (rest tucked away). Delete out of the primary set. Keep View timeline as the bridge back. Surface the credential as trust. Stay unmistakably Full Ride.

PICK ONE All three answer the brief — they differ in how loud, and what the badge “is”
