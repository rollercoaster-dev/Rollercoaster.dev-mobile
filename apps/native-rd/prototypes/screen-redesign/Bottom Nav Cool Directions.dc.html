A SWITCH WORTH TOUCHING.

Settled: Focus Mode is reached only by picking a goal — it’s never a nav destination — so the bar is just a switch between the three stacks. “+ new goal” moves into the Goals list. No resume guesswork, nothing decided for the user. That frees the bar to be pure feel. Three physical, neo-brutalist takes — each shown switched to all three destinations so the mechanic reads.

Through-line color system — Goals = yellow   Badges = mint   Settings = white

The Slide

Evolves the signature half-lift + morph into a physical fader. A chunky accent knob carries the active label and snaps along a track between slots — a hard slide, no opacity fade. Reduce-motion just jumps it. Keeps the pill breaking above the slot, so it stays unmistakably Rollercoaster.

Press Keys

lowest motion

Three fat keys, like a chunky physical keypad. The active key is pressed in — it drops into its own shadow offset and flattens, taking its destination color. No travel, no fade; the state is the depth. All three labels always visible — the most predictable, lowest-motion take. Sits flat (no lift).

Marquee Tape

loudest

A row of icon chips, and the active destination stamps a big angled label-tape above them — like a label-maker strip slapped on, in that destination’s color. You always read a loud, confident GOALS / BADGES / SETTINGS. The tape is the lifted element; switching re-stamps it with a quick settle. Maximal Full Ride personality.
