Phase 1 · Directions for the finishing flow

CELEBRATE, THEN CRAFT THE KEEPSAKE.

Three ways to cut the finishing flow down — all keeping a real pause to design the badge as the reward. By now they’ve done the work and seen the whole journey, so the designer arrives with context: this is the moment to make something that fits this win. Everything redundant around it is stripped.

All three keep
• A deliberate badge-design pause, framed as the reward
• The designer seeing the journey’s context (goal, steps, evidence)
• One clean badge reveal at the end → Badge Detail
All three cut
• The separate evidence-prompt screen (note goes inline)
• The duplicate BadgeEarnedModal — the reveal is the screen
• The manual “Bake It” tap — finishing the design bakes it
• Errors/spinners out of the celebratory card

My lean · A
You want the design to feel like an earned reward with the journey’s context behind it — A gives that its own breath: a clean “you did it” beat, then a dedicated craft screen, then the reveal. B is the tighter cut if you’d rather not add a transition. C trades the “craft from a blank-ish slate” intent for instant gratification — good if some users won’t care to design, but it makes the pause optional.
