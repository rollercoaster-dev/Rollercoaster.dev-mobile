A goal is named here, then you land in the steps. There is no step-level detail at create time at all — dates and dependencies have no home on either surface.
⚠ The gap
A step row carries exactly two things today: a title and planned evidence types. There is no field, chip, or sheet anywhere to set after <step>, waiting on <someone>, or a due date — the truth-lines the Timeline renders have no way to be authored.
The brief
Add a quiet, optional second gesture on a step you’ve already named — one tap from the row, defaulting to nothing. It must never make a plain step feel unfinished, never block or dim the step, and produce exactly the inline lines Focus/Timeline already show.
DIRECTION A One ghost affordance → a single “Add detail” sheet
Every step gets one dashed, skippable row: ＋ timing & dependencies. It opens a single bottom sheet with all three controls in one place. 1 tap to open, set any combination, done. Best when a step often carries more than one line.

CARRIED QUESTIONS Resolved the same way in all three
C-expected vs B-due (Q10). Always two separate controls, two separate lines, two palettes (amber expected = the world’s timing; neutral due = your intent). Setting one never fills the other.
Past expected date. Recommendation: a passed date reads neutral past-tense — was expected Jun 12 — factual, never “late” / “overdue,” and the complete action stays live throughout. (Carried into the final to confirm.)
B-soft vs B-firm. The date control offers Sometime (soft intent) vs a real date — the lightest split that expresses both. Repetition is noted as a later third option, not built now.
