What needs cleaning
1
Two “moments” back to back
🏆 “One last thing!” then 🎯 “You did it!” — two emoji, two headlines, two screens for one finish. The evidence prompt isn’t a celebration; it dilutes the one that follows.
2
The badge appears 4 times
Preview on celebrate → dimmed while baking → again in the earned modal → again in Badge Detail. The reveal loses its punch by the third repeat.
3
Modal duplicates the screen
BadgeEarnedModal shows the badge and a “View Badge” button — the celebration screen behind it does the same job. One of them should go.
4
“Add evidence” twice
Screen 2 is entirely about capturing evidence; screen 6 brings back “Add Final Evidence.” Same action, two places, opposite sides of the celebration.
5
System status in the moment
Baking spinner, “signing key unavailable,” error text and a Retry button all live inside the same celebratory card. Failure and confetti shouldn’t share a frame.
6
Manual bake + redesign branch
Bake never auto-fires, and “Redesign First” throws the user into the full 5-section designer mid-celebration. Both add taps and a decision exactly when momentum should carry them.
7
Too many exits, no clear one
Across the modal and screen 6: View Badge, Keep going, Add Final Evidence, View Your Journey, Reopen Goal. Five ways out, no obvious single next step.
8
Unpredictable entry
Whether you land on the evidence prompt or jump straight to celebrate depends on whether evidence already exists. The first thing you see changes run to run.
Calls to make before we redesign
1. One screen or two? — Fold the evidence prompt into the celebration (optional “add a note” inline), or keep a distinct “capture proof” step before the reveal?
2. Keep the earned modal? — The celebration screen and the modal overlap. Drop the modal and let the screen be the moment, or drop the screen and let bake → modal → Badge Detail carry it?
3. Auto-bake? — Bake silently the instant they finish (badge just appears), or keep an explicit “Bake It” tap?
4. Where does redesign live? — Remove “Redesign First” from completion and let people tweak from Badge Detail afterward, or keep an in-the-moment edit?
5. The single next step — after the badge is earned, what is the button? View Badge, See your journey, or back to Goals?
Decide these and the redesign collapses to a much shorter flow — likely one celebration screen → Badge Detail, with evidence and redesign as optional side-doors rather than mandatory stops.
